Welcome Members of IoRT

Teams to choose from

  1. ROS2 Development and Deployment Team

    This team focuses on building and running Robot Operating System (ROS 2) nodes, packages, and systems. They handle communication between various sensors, actuators, and other robotic components.ROS 2 allows the creation of scalable, modular, and reusable software stacks for robots. It's essential for managing communication in robotics systems, especially in complex mobile robots.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • To understand the problem the robot is trying to solve and decide on the potential nodes to accomplish the desired outcome.
    • Develop and maintain ROS 2 packages for various robots.
    • Integrate hardware components with ROS 2 (sensors, actuators, motors).
    • Create algorithms for autonomous navigation, manipulation, and perception using packages like SLAM and Moveit.

  2. Electronics Design and Simulation Team (Proteus/MATLAB/Simulink)

    This team handles the design and simulation of electronic circuits. They focus on developing the circuitry that controls robots, including motor drivers, power management with or without using BMS, and sensor integration. Electronics form the core of every robot. Accurate circuit designs and simulations reduce hardware debugging, saving time and resources.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Design and simulate electronic circuits using tools like Proteus, MATLAB, and Simulink.
    • Create motor driver circuits, sensor integration modules, and power management systems.
    • Simulate circuits to ensure functionality before hardware implementation.
    • Provide technical support to other teams regarding power systems, embedded systems, and sensors.

  3. Mechanical Design and 3D Modeling Team (Fusion 360/SolidWorks)

    This team focuses on the mechanical aspects of robot design, including structural components, joints, and linkages. They create 3D models and mechanical assemblies. Robots need a robust physical structure to operate in the real world. Mechanical design is crucial to ensure functionality, stability, and mobility.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Design 3D models and assemblies of robots and their components using Fusion 360 or SolidWorks.
    • Develop parts for fabrication using 3D printing, CNC, or laser cutting.
    • Simulate the stress and dynamics of robotic components using Ansys.
    • Collaborate with the ROS 2 and Electronics teams to ensure seamless integration between software and hardware.
    • Collaborate with Gazebo team to analyse your models efficiency on earth conditions.

  4. Gazebo Simulation Team

    What it's all about: This team works on setting up robotic simulations in Gazebo. They create virtual environments where robots can be tested and iterated upon before building physical prototypes. Usefulness: Simulation allows you to test robots in complex environments, saving time and costs in physical prototyping.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Develop and maintain simulation environments in Gazebo.
    • Integrate robot URDF/Xacro models with the simulation.
    • Test robotic movements, navigation algorithms, and obstacle detection in virtual environments.
    • Optimize simulations to reflect real-world conditions as closely as possible.
    • Collaborate with Mechanical team to analyse your models efficiency on earth conditions.

  5. Embedded Systems Team (Arduino/ESP32/Raspberry Pi)

    What it's all about: This team specializes in the firmware and low-level programming of microcontrollers such as Arduino, ESP32, or Raspberry PI. They handle real-time operations like sensor data collection and motor control. Usefulness: Embedded systems are the backbone of robotics control. These controllers help in executing the low-level commands issued by ROS 2 or other software.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Program microcontrollers for sensor interfacing and motor control.
    • Optimize code for real-time performance and reliability.
    • Work on communication protocols (I2C, SPI, UART, MQTT, CAN).
    • Collaborate with the ROS 2 team to bridge communication between embedded systems and higher-level robotics software.

  6. Computer Vision and AI Team (OpenCV/YOLO/TensorFlow)

    What it's all about: This team is responsible for developing vision-based algorithms for object detection, tracking, and navigation using tools like OpenCV, YOLO, or TensorFlow. Usefulness: Vision systems are key for autonomous robots, enabling tasks like object recognition, obstacle avoidance, and map generation.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Develop algorithms for computer vision tasks like object recognition, depth sensing, and visual odometry.
    • Integrate vision systems with ROS 2 for real-time navigation and object interaction.
    • Optimize AI models for performance on embedded platforms like the Upsquared or Raspberry Pi.
    • Test algorithms in simulation and real-world environments.

  7. Control Systems and Kinematics Team

    What it's all about: This team works on the control algorithms and mathematical models that govern the movement and behavior of robots, such as PID control, kinematics, and dynamics. Usefulness: Precise control systems are vital for smooth, accurate movements in robotic arms, mobile robots, and drones.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Develop control algorithms for robot arms, mobile robots, and other actuated systems.
    • Work on inverse kinematics, forward kinematics, and dynamics calculations.
    • Implement PID controllers, motion planning algorithms, and sensor fusion techniques.
    • Collaborate with the ROS 2 team to implement these algorithms on real-time robotic systems.

  8. Backend and Networking Team (Node.js/MQTT/HTTP/IoT)

    What it's all about: This team is responsible for developing backend services and communication protocols that allow remote control and monitoring of robots. They manage databases, cloud integration, and real-time communication. Usefulness: Robots often need to communicate with cloud systems, user interfaces, or other robots, making efficient backend and networking essential.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Develop web-based interfaces for robot control using Node.js or Flask.
    • Implement real-time communication protocols (MQTT, HTTP, WebSocket) for remote robot management.
    • Create IoT dashboards to monitor sensor data, performance, and battery levels.
    • Collaborate with the Embedded and ROS 2 teams to ensure seamless communication between hardware, software, and the cloud.

  9. Documentation and Research Team

    What it's all about: This team focuses on maintaining comprehensive documentation for all projects and conducting research on the latest robotics technologies. Usefulness: Proper documentation ensures that knowledge is retained within the team, and research allows the club to stay on the cutting edge of robotics.

    The responsibilities of the members of this team include:

    • Document hardware and software design, installation guides, and troubleshooting steps.
    • Conduct research on the latest advancements in robotics, such as AI, control systems, and sensors.
    • Create technical reports, presentations, and tutorials for the club.
    • Maintain a shared repository of all projects for future reference.
